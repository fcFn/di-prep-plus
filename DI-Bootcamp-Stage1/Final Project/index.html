David Rolfe Graeber (/ˈɡreɪbər/; February 12, 1961 – September 2, 2020) was an American anthropologist and anarchist activist. His influential work in economic anthropology, particularly his books Debt: The First 5,000 Years (2011), Bullshit Jobs (2018), and The Dawn of Everything (2021), and his leading role in the Occupy movement, earned him recognition as one of the foremost anthropologists and left-wing thinkers of his time time (source: Wikipedia).

For me personally, David Graeber had shown that the world of today is not necessarily the best possible world through his activism and his published works. His last book, The Dawn of Everything, shows that perhaps we have misunderstood so many things about humanity's past, present and future.

His other works contain many radical ideas that challenge the status quo. To me David Graeber was a true visionary.

Important events

February 12, 1961

Born in New York to a working-class Jewish family.

1996

Obtains a PhD in anthropology from the University of Chicago. His doctoral thesis, "The Disastrous Ordeal of 1987: Memory and Violence in Rural Madagascar", was later adapted into a book-length ethnographic study, "Lost People: Magic and the Legacy of Slave in Madagascar".

1998

Graeber begins teaching at Yale University, where he remains until 2005.

2001

Graeber's first book, "Toward an Anthropological Theory of Value: The False Coin of Our Own Dreams", is published.

2004

Graeber publishes "Fragments of an Anarchist Anthropology", a short book that outlines his vision of anarchism.

2005

Leaves Yale University after his contract is not renewed. His "academic exile" begins. He's unable to find another position in any of the major universities in the United States.

2008

Begins teaching at the University of London's Goldsmiths College.

2011

Takes a leadership role in the Occupy Wall Street movement.

2011

Publishes "Debt: The First 5,000 Years", a book that explores the history of debt and credit from an anarchist and anthropological perspectives.

2013

His essay "On the Phenomenon of Bullshit Jobs" goes viral. It will be adapted into a book, "Bullshit Jobs: A Theory", in 2018.

2015

The book "The Utopia of Rules: On Technology, Stupidity, and the Secret Joys of Bureaucracy" is published. It explores the role of bureaucracy in modern society.

2019

Marries the artist and writer Nika Dubrovsky.

2020

David suddenly dies in Venice, Italy, on September 2, 2020 from necrotic pancreatitis. He was 59 years old.

2021

The Dawn of Everything: A New History of Humanity is published posthumously. The book, co-authored with David Wengrow, challenges many assumptions about the origins of civilization.
